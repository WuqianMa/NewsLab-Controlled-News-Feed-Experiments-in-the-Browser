# NewsLab function tour script

Target length: about 62 seconds. The video demonstrates software functions only. It makes no claim about the scientific value of the seeded content or example measurements. Full narration subtitles are burned into the MP4, with an editable `.srt` copy beside it. Scenes use stable holds and narration-timed cuts without camera movement or overlapping transitions.

## Narration

1. NewsLab turns a research plan into a controlled, browser-based news-feed study.
2. Researchers configure assignment, sample targets, consent, completion, and condition-specific feed controls in one experiment workspace.
3. Articles become approved content sets, with direct control over the sequence delivered to each condition.
4. Optional AI providers can propose article variants. Every result enters a side-by-side human review before approval.
5. Participants enter through study-specific consent, then continue into the experience assigned to their condition.
6. The responsive feed supports scrolling, article views, and like, share, or save controls while recording each interaction.
7. The configured survey follows naturally, supporting Likert, choice, and optional free-text questions.
8. Back in the researcher workspace, participant and session summaries show progress while keeping preview records separate.
9. Finally, NewsLab exports events, participants, exposures, and survey responses as structured CSV files with a data dictionary. Researchers define the questions; NewsLab runs the workflow.

## Scene order

1. Researcher dashboard
2. Experiment configuration
3. Content library and ordered sets
4. Variant generation and review
5. Participant consent
6. Participant feed
7. Post-feed survey
8. Participant/session monitor
9. Structured exports and closing message
